# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Live sound volunteers, streamers, and audio engineers who need to set and monitor gain quickly in a session with many active sources. They are usually working under time pressure, with limited attention, and may be glancing at the plugin from across a dim room.

## Product Purpose

STRATA is a zero-latency gain-staging and utility channel strip. It helps users set healthy input gain, read output level at a glance, mute or bypass quickly, and coordinate multiple plugin instances from a shared Channel View. Success means the operator can trust the levels, move fast, and avoid clipping or guesswork.

## Brand Personality

Terse, technical, and calm under pressure. STRATA should feel like pro audio hardware, not consumer software. The tone is confident, functional, and almost silent, with the visual language doing the work instead of marketing copy. Three words: readable, precise, stage-ready.

## Anti-references

- Glossy consumer audio apps with decorative gradients, chrome, or playful motion.
- SaaS dashboards that feel abstract, soft, or overly friendly.
- Busy control panels that bury state under labels, panels, and dense copy.
- Retro analog nostalgia that looks nostalgic first and functional second.
- Thin, low-contrast UI that only works when viewed up close.

## Design Principles

1. Make state legible instantly, especially clipping, bypass, linking, and group membership.
2. Keep the interface compact and console-like so it reads as a tool, not a webpage.
3. Use color semantically and consistently so the same hue means the same thing everywhere.
4. Prioritize speed of interaction over ornament, with controls that are obvious under stress.
5. Preserve enough physicality in the meter and fader to feel like real audio hardware, but keep the system restrained and utilitarian.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target strong contrast, large hit targets, and fast recognition at a distance. Support reduced motion where possible. Do not rely on color alone to communicate important state, and keep labels and numeric readouts readable for users with low-light, color-vision, or attention constraints.
